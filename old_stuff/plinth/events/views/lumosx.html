Lumos

Light is something we all crave for, something we all seek. It is time for robots to express those same desires. In this autonomous event there are no guides -­‐ no lines or walls or objects to look to. The only way forward is to perceive the luminosity of sources scattered around the arena, in an attempt to switch off as many as possible before time runs out.
Insisto Lux Lucis. Follow the Light.



Build autonomous robot that can differentiate between a number of light sources of similar intensity kept inside an isolated room (Arena) and turn each one off.

Task

The robot has to begin its run from a predefined position in a dark isolated room with the light sources kept in different directions w.r.t. the initial position of the robot. There will be no guiding path on the floor of the arena. The robot has to decide its path by light detection only. The objective is to switch off the maximum number of light sources by the end of the runtime.

Round 1 :
  1. There will be a total of 5 light sources placed in the room.
  2. All the light sources will be stationary.
  3. The total runtime for this round is 5 minutes.
Round 2 :
  1. There will be a total of 6 light sources placed in the room:
    • 4 of the light sources will be Stationary.
    • 2 of the light sources will be moving at a maximum speed of 4 cm/s.
  2. The total runtime for this round is 7 minutes.
Arena
  1. The light sources will be placed at any random positions in the arena.
  2. The actual positions of the light sources in the arena will be disclosed just before the commencement of the run.
  3. The arena will be enclosed by walls on all sides. The walls will be covered by black cloth.
Round 1 :
  1. The arena is an enclosed circular area of radius 3.0m.
  2. The starting position will be a 60 cm sided square in the arena.
Round 2:
  1. The arena is an enclosed circular area of radius 3.0m.
  2. The starting position will be the same as in the first round.
Light Sources :
  1. Each light source will be a cylindrical setup of height 25 cm and diameter 25
    2. cm.
  2. The intensity distribution of the each light source measured individually, in a
    3. completely dark room, will be nearly the same.
  3. All dimensions have a tolerance of 15 %.
  4. The rating of the bulb to be used for all light sources is 100 Watt, 220 V.

General Rules
  1. No abstract submission is required for the event.
  2. Each team can consist of a maximum of 4 members.
  3. Each team should have unique participants i.e. no two teams can have even any common participant.
  4. The team members can be from different institutes or colleges.
  5. The right spirit of participation is expected from the participants.
  6. The decision of Team PLINTH will be final and binding on all participants.
Event
Stationary sources
  1. There would be stationary light sources present in the arena in both the
    2. rounds.
  2. The intensity distribution of the each light source measured individually, in a completely dark room, will be nearly the same.
  3. The robot has to reach each light source and bump into the metal plates present around each light source. This would deactivate the light source i.e. it would be switched off.
  4. The light source is considered to be deactivated only if it is switched off. In case a robot collides and the source is still illuminated it would be considered as an active light source.
Moving sources
  1. There would be two moving light sources present in the arena during the second round of the event.
  2. The moving light sources would be autonomous having pre-­‐defined algorithm and they would be moving in an unknown path.
  3. The moving light sources can be deactivated in the same way as stationary light sources.
  4. The moving sources will stop automatically upon deactivation.
Traversal
  1. The robot must avoid colliding with already deactivated sources as it traverses towards another light source. Collision with any deactivated light source will lead to a penalty.
  2. For the time bonus to be rewarded, it is compulsory to deactivate at least two (2) light sources before the end of the run, in case the participant wishes to call off the run before the allotted run time.
Initial Orientation of Robot
  1. The participant is allowed to decide the initial orientation of his robot but it should be such that it is not facing any light source directly.
  2. Team PLINTH reserves the right to disallow any initial orientation of the robot inside the starting zone if it gives the participant an undue advantage solely based on the judge's discretion.
Restarts & Timeouts
  1. A maximum of 2 Timeouts of 2 minutes each may be taken in the first round. For the second round a maximum of 3 Timeouts of 2 minutes each may be taken.
  2. In both the rounds, a Penalty will be awarded for each timeout and the robot will start from the last light source it has deactivated, if none have been deactivated then from the start zone. In case of multiple robot, this rule is applicable to each of the robot individually.
  3. The participant's robot can have a maximum of three restarts in both the round. Restarts, if any, will apply to all the robot. A penalty will be imposed on the team for every restart that they take.
  4. In case of a restart the participant's robot will be set to their initial positions. Timer will be set to zero and the run will start afresh with the addition of the penalty for restart.
Robot Specifications
  1. The robot must fit inside a cube of side 55cm when measured. No part/mechanism of/on the robot should exceed the given dimensions before the commencement of the event run. The robot can exceed their respective dimensions once the event commences.
  2. The autonomous robot should be On‐board processing robot, i.e., the robot cannot be controlled by a remotely kept computer. The Processor used for on the robot must be at maximum 16 bit processor.
  3. LEGO kits or its spare parts or pre‐made mechanical parts are not allowed.
  4. Ready--made gearboxes, sensors, development boards can be used but no other part of the robot should contain any ready‐made components. Simple car bases with no extra features may be used.
  5. The robot should not harm the arena in any way. If it does so, a penalty will be imposed on the team. The magnitude of the penalty will be decided by Team PLINTH.
Technical
  1. All the dimensions of the arena and light sources are to be considered with a tolerance of 15%.
  2. The robot can be powered on-board as well as off‐board.
  3. No kind of external control will be allowed.
  4. All circuitry and sensory equipment should be placed on the robot adhering
    5. to the ROBOT SPECIFICATIONS.
  5. Participants will have to bring their own programmers, cables and software. No programmers will be provided.
Scoring
  1. Rewards
    1. No. of stationary light sources Deactivated : (S)
    2. No. of moving light sources Deactivated: (M)
    3. Time Bonus: {B = (Total Time-­‐ Run Time)/2}
  2. Penalties
    1. No. of times the robot again hits an already deactivated light source: (D)
    2. No. of Timeouts taken: (T)
    3. No. of Restarts taken: (R).
Final Score = 500 + (S*80) + (M*120) + (B) – (D*30) – (T*20) – (R*50).
